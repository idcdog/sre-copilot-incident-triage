## Design Context

### Users

Primary users are Devpost judges, SRE/DevOps engineers, platform engineers, and technical leaders evaluating an AI-assisted incident triage concept. They need to understand the RCA workflow quickly, trust that the demo is synthetic and safe, and see how multiple specialist agents collaborate across common production architecture layers.

### Brand Personality

Controlled, evidence-driven, cinematic. The interface should feel like a serious operations control plane rather than a generic dashboard. It should communicate confidence, auditability, and technical depth without implying autonomous production changes.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a dark industrial SRE control-room style inspired by the existing AI SRE architecture site: deep green-black background, fine grid lines, mint evidence highlights, radar/topology motifs, precise mono labels, large editorial typography, and purposeful motion. Avoid playful mascots, generic SaaS cards, purple/cyan AI gradients, and decorative animation that does not explain the workflow.

### Design Principles

1. Evidence first: every animation should make signal flow, causality, confidence, or approval boundaries easier to understand.
2. Controlled execution: read-only investigation and approval-gated actions must remain visually distinct.
3. Cinematic but legible: the topology should feel impressive, but labels, stages, and conclusions must remain readable.
4. Synthetic by default: all nodes, incidents, services, and telemetry are mock data based on common company architecture patterns.
5. Reduced motion respected: the experience must provide a usable static fallback for motion-sensitive users.
